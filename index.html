Avocado Purée

4 avocados (2 cups purée)

4 bunches cilantro (4 cups loosely packed)

1/2 cup rice vinegar

1/2 cup mirin

1 cup lime juice

2 tsp salt

1 Tbsp ground black pepper

  • Add all ingredients into blender except avocados and blend on high until liquified.
  • Add avocados and blend until smooth.
  • Transfer into a bottle, storage container, or ziploc bag (to freeze).
    • Chili Oil

    1 cup roughly choped chili arbol

    4 cups frying oil

  • Place chili flakes and oil in a cooking pot.
  • Cook on high heat until the temperature reaches 230º F.
  • Remove from heat immediately.
  • Strain the oil through a sieve into a metal container to remove the chili flakes.
  • Strain the oil through a coffee/tea filter into a metal container to remove smaller particles.
  • Let the oil cool to touch and then transfer it into a storage container.
    • Chili Ponzu

    2 cups ponzu sauce

    1/4 cup sriracha

    1/4 cup chili oil

  • Mix all ingredient together in a bowl.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Garlic Grapeseed Oil

    4 cups grapeseed oil

    1 cup garlic cloves

  • Place garlic cloves and oil in a cooking pot.
  • Cook on high heat until the temperature reaches 230º F.
  • Remove from heat immediately.
  • Strain the oil through a sieve into a metal container to remove the garli.
  • Strain the oil through a coffee/tea filter into a metal container to remove smaller particles.
  • Let the oil cool to touch and then transfer it into a storage container.
    • Garlic Ponzu

    8 cups ponzu sauce

    320 grams garlic cloves

    8 grams vegetable oil

  • Pre heat the oven to 375°F
  • Toss garlic with oil to coat evenly.
  • Wrap garlic in a flat even square shape and place on a sheet pan.
  • Place it in the oven on a middle rack.
  • Roast for 15 minutes.
  • Flip over once and roast for another 15 to 25 minutes until soft.
  • Remove from oven and place in a blender while still hot.
  • Add a small amount of ponzu and blend until smooth.
  • Keep adding ponzu and blending until fully mixed
  • Strain the sauce through a fine mesh sieve.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Kombu Dashi Stock

    4 liters water

    3 ounces kombu (kelp)

    3 ounces bonito flakes

    200 ml cold water

  • Soak kombu in 4 liters of water for 5 hours (can be left overnight or quickly for 1 hour).
  • Remove kombu.
  • Bring "kombu" water to a boil.
  • Remove from heat.
  • Add 200 ml cold water.
  • Add bonito flakes.
  • Let soak for 15 minutes.
  • Strain through a fine mesh sieve (do not press).
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Lemon Dill Aioli

    16 cups mayonnaise

    2 cups lemon juice

    3/4 cup yuzu juice

    1 1/2 cups dry dill weed

  • Add all ingredients into a bowl and mix together.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Meesh Mash

    2 cups pickled ginger (gari)

    2 cups low sodium soy sauce

    2 cups garlic ponzu sauce

    6 ounces truffle oil

  • Add all ingredients except truffle oil into a blender and blend on high until it is puréed.
  • Strain and press through a medium mesh sieve so that some of the purée is retained in the sauce for thickness.
  • Add the 6 ounces of truffle oil and mix.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Pineapple Rum

    1 (64) ounce can of pineapple juice

    1/2 cup white granulated white sugar

    2 ounces of rum

    Zest from 2 limes

    Corn starch slurry to thicken

  • Add pineapple juice and sugar into a sauce pot and boil for 2 minutes.
  • Add 2 ounces of rum and boil for 1 minute.
  • Add corn starch slurry and boil for 2 minutes.
  • Transfer into a storage container and mix in the lime zest.
    • Poki

    4 cups ponzu sauce

    1/2 cup sesame oil

    1/2 cup chili oil

    1/2 cup sriracha

    1 gram xanthan gum

  • Add 1/2 cup sesame oil, 1/4 cup sriracha and 1/2 gram of xanthan gum into a cup and blend with a hand blender until thick.
  • Add 1/2 cup chili oil, 1/4 cup sriracha and 1/2 gram of xanthan gum into a cup and blend with a hand blender until thick.
  • Add both into a larger container and slowly blend in the ponzu until full incorporated.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Ponzu

    300 ml dashi kombu stock

    300 ml rice vinegar (shiragiku)

    100 ml mirin

    200 ml lemon juice

    100 ml yuzu juice

    300 ml soy sauce

  • Combine all ingredients in storage container and whisk.
    • Sesame Olive Oil

    1 part sesame oil

    2 parts extra virgin olive oil

    ***You can substitue "parts" for whatever works for you. (ex. 1 cup, 2 cups)***
  • Combine all the ingredients and transfer to a bottle or storage container.
    • Shari Awase Zu

    12 liters rice vinegar (shiragiku)

    2 liters lees "darker" vinegar (mizkan)

    12 pounds of granulated white sugar

    2 pounds kosher salt

    3 ounces of dashi kombu (kelp)

  • Add both vinegars to a large mixing pot and bring to a boil.
  • Add the sugar and bring to a boil for 2 minutes.
  • Place the kombu in a large storage container.
  • Carefully pour the combined vinegar on to the kombu.
  • Cover with plastic wrap and poke holes in the plastic wrap.
  • Let cool over night.
    • Spicy Mayo

    3 gallons of mayonnaise

    6 bottles of sriracha (13 cups)

    3 cups of sesame oil

    1/2 cup lemon juice

    1/2 cup lime juice

  • Combine all ingredients together into a large mixing bowl.
  • Whisk until fully mixed.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Sweet Evil

    4 cups ponzu sauce

    4 cups eel sauce

    4 cups dried chili de arbol

    1 cup sriracha sauce

  • Combine all ingredients except chili de arbol into a container to form the liquid mixture.
  • Add the chili de arbol and a small amount of the liquid mixture into a blender and grind into a paste.
  • Slowly incorporate as much of the liquid mixture into the blender as you can.
  • Pour some of the sauce into a storage container to make room for the remaining liquid.
  • Blend the rest of the sauce together.
  • Transfer into a storage container and whisk together.
    • Wasabi Mayo

    4 cups mayonnaise

    1 cup wasabi paste

    1/2 cup honey

    ***Warm honey in hot water before using.***
  • Combine the wasabi paste and 1 cup of the mayonnaise in a mixing bowl.
  • Combine together with the rest of the mayonnaise.
  • Add in the honey and mix together.
  • Transfer into a storage container.
    • Yuzu Soy

    10 parts low sodium soy sauce

    4 parts yuzu juice

    ***You can substitue "parts" for whatever works for you. (ex. 1 cup, 2 cups)***
  • Combine all the ingredients and transfer to a bottle or storage container.
